Terms 	Definitions
Costing	The process of estimate overhead costs related to production and then produces the P\L status 
ABC	Activity-based costing is a system that attempts to accurately trace indirect costs to products by allocating indirect costs to activities and then to products based on their usage of the activities.(used when indirect cost > direct cost or where the Accuracy required)
Cost Driver	A cost driver is the unit of an activity that causes the change in activity's cost.
Price Point	Price points are prices at which demand for a given product is supposed to stay relatively high.
Fixed Cost	Fixed cost are the costs that are not dependent on the level of goods or services produced by the business.(there are no fixed costs in the long run)
Variable Cost	Variable costs are costs that change in proportion to the good or service that a business produces(often related to direct cost)
Overhead	Overhead expense refers to an ongoing expense of operating a business(often related to Fixed Cost or indirect cost, do not directly generate profits but still vital to business operations)
Cost Auditing	It is the verification of cost accounting records to ensure that they are in conformity with Cost Accounting principles, plans, procedures and objective.
Man-Hour	is the amount of work performed by the average worker in one hour.
